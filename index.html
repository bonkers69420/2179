The Two Malaysias:
A Nation Growing, But Not Equally

Section 01

General Statistics

Income has grown

From 1970 to 2022, the median and mean income of Malaysian households have increased in a comfortable rate with the biggest leap in absolute value happening around the 2010s.

However, this took a turn for the worse during 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic which have left the world in a lockdown. Until this day, many countries are still recovering from the fallout.

Growth rate over the years

Malaysia experienced strong economic growth in 1983 and 1984 due to increased exports of crude petroleum and electronics, which became major contributors to GDP growth, resulting in increased income.

During the year 2020, the world was faced with the COVID-19 pandemic, halting many economic activities. This results in an immense decrease in income for households in Malaysia.

How about the states?

Across the decades, Selangor has remained at the top, never falling below 1st place. Kelantan remains consistently in last place.

Terengganu is the state that has seen the most drastic change across the decade. For the first 3 decades, it has consistenly remained at the bottom. However, in the last 2 decades, it has taken over and in 2022 it is ranked the 5th highest.

Section 02

A Closer Look: The State of Malaysia in 2022

Does poverty rate have a effect on net cash flow?

Poverty rate refers to the ratio of the population who falls below the poverty line. Kelantan and Sabah are areas that have a higher poverty rate. This can be due to the lack of urbanisation in those areas or due to the difference in cost of living in different states.

Breaking it down further

From observation, we can see that the cost of living in Selangor is the highest among the states at RM 6770. However, Selangor is a well developed area, resulting in many job opportunities. As such, it also has the highest mean income. Whereas, Kelantan suffers from not only a higher poverty rate but also a higher expenditure than other states which share a high poverty rate.

This results in Selangor having the largest net cash flow. However, not all states with a high poverty rate would have the lowest net cash flow. Kelantan is unfortunate to suffer from both a high poverty rate and high expenditure without sufficient income. Other states such as Sabah and Sarawak does not suffer from a low net cash flow. As such, poverty rate does not have a correlation with net cash flow.

Inequality, Poverty & The Cost of Living

Inequality and poverty move together

Poverty rate and Gini coefficient has shrunk over the years. This shows that Malaysians have succeeded in meeting their basic needs.

It can be observed that income growth rate is volatile whereas the inflation rate remains low. Income growth usually outpaces inflation rate, indicating a healthy economy.
